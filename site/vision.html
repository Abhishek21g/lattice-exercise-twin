The problem, in plain English

Anduril teams run formation exercises on Lattice — multiple nodes sharing tracks and tasks over a mesh. The integration test in sandbox looks fine. Then something breaks hours into the live exercise.

Sandbox passes. Hour 14 doesn't.

What goes wrong

Imagine three nodes: a sensor tower, a relay, and a UAV at the edge. The sensor sees a hostile track and publishes it on the Entities stream. Your app listens and assigns an Investigate task to the nearest asset.

In the sandbox, all three nodes are always online. Tasks assign. Tests pass. You ship.

In a 72-hour field exercise, relay-3 dies at hour 14. The UAV stops getting entity updates. Investigate tasks stop assigning. Nobody notices until the exercise is already broken.

What this tool does

Exercise Fails First (Lattice Exercise Twin) simulates that exercise in software before you fly. You define your mesh, pick a failure scenario, and get a clear verdict: DEPLOY or BLOCK.

  • plan — load your mesh YAML
  • run — simulate 72 hours with optional node failures
  • doctor — read the verdict receipt
  • report — export HTML timeline for your team

Where it comes from

Built from Anduril's public sample-app-auto-reconnaissance pattern: stream entities, assign Investigate when a track is near. Mock mode runs without Lattice credentials. Live mode connects to your sandbox when you're ready.
